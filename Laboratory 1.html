Ice Cream

Ice cream is a sweetened frozen food typically eaten as a snack or dessert. It may be made from milk or cream and is flavoured with a sweetener, either sugar or an alternative, and a spice, such as cocoa or vanilla, or with fruit such as strawberries or peaches. It can also be made by whisking a flavored cream base and liquid nitrogen together. Food coloring is sometimes added, in addition to stabilizers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Quart Milk
  • 200 grams Cream
  • 1-2 drops Yellow Color
  • 12-tbsp. Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Milk Powder
  • 1-tsp. Corn Flour
  • 1-tsp. Vanilla Extract
  • 1-tbsp. Margarine or Butter

Preparation:

Heat milk to a boil. Mix milk powder and corn flour in one cup cold milk. Add this to the boiled milk. When cool add sugar, butter, cream and churn. Add extract. Put in an airtight container. Keep in a deep freezer for 4-6 hours. Remove and let it melt. Put this liquid in an aluminum flat container. Keep in a deep freezer again until it is frozen completely.
